2025 Clinical Research Special Edition: Analyzing Pathological Mechanisms of Acute Gastritis via Multimodal Inference

Chief Lecturer: Junqi Ding | NDHU EE-CSIE Medical AI Lab | Status: Peer Reviewed

I. Abstract & Introduction

This study presents a systematic mechanism analysis of the Norovirus GII.4 variants. Clinical observations indicate that pathogen-mediated inflammatory pathways have evolved from simple mucosal damage into complex multi-organ stress responses. Lead Lecturer Ding's team utilized high-throughput sequencing and molecular dynamics simulations to demonstrate, for the first time, that inflammatory factors directly interfere with AQP3 channels in intestinal epithelial cells, triggering quantum-level osmotic imbalances.

II. Technical & Neural Architecture

// Pathological Signal Inference Logic
The model utilizes a Transformer-based TFE architecture, introducing Multi-Head Self-Attention for temporal feature fusion. NVIDIA CUDA 12.2 was employed for non-linear tensor optimization to predict AQP3 protein expression anomalies accurately.

1. TensorFlow 2.15+ (XNNPACK Quantization)
2. Scipy DWT (Discrete Wavelet Transform)
3. NVIDIA TensorRT Acceleration
